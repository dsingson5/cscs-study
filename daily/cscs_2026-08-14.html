Bioenergetics
Deep ReviewExercise ScienceCh 3 (5E) · 5E Ch 3 — Energy Production & Capacity; Substrate Depletion & Repletion; Oxygen Uptake / EPOC; Metabolic Specificity & Interval Training

Energy system interaction & substrate use

All three systems (phosphagen, glycolytic, oxidative) run at once; contribution is set primarily by intensity, secondarily by duration. Rate and capacity trade off inversely: phosphagen = fastest rate/lowest capacity, fat oxidation = slowest rate/highest capacity. Fatigue tracks depletion of phosphagens and glycogen — not FFAs, lactate, or amino acids. At exercise onset an O₂ deficit is covered anaerobically; afterward EPOC (excess post-exercise O₂ consumption) repays it, restoring PCr/ATP, reloading O₂ in blood and muscle, and clearing metabolites. EPOC scales mainly with intensity. Manipulating work:rest ratios lets you 'select' a target energy system.

Key facts to memorize
  • Duration→primary system: 0-6s phosphagen; 6-30s phosphagen+fast glycolysis; 30s-2min fast glycolysis; 2-3min slow glycolysis+oxidative; >3min oxidative
  • Rate rank (fastest→slowest): phosphagen > fast glycolysis > slow glycolysis > CHO oxidation > fat/protein oxidation; capacity is the inverse order
  • PCr can be near-fully depleted by intense exercise; ATP holds up (may drop ≤50-60%) because CP + myokinase + oxidation defend it
  • PCr repletion: ~half back in 30s; full ATP in ~3-5min; full CP by ~8min — chiefly aerobic, so hard efforts need long work:rest
  • Muscle glycogen ~300-400g total; liver ~70-100g; glycogenolysis rate rises with intensity (0.7 / 1.4 / 3.4 mmol·kg⁻¹·min⁻¹ at 50/75/100% VO₂max)
  • Glycogen repletion: 0.7-3.0 g CHO/kg every 2h → 5-6 mmol/g wet muscle/h for 4-6h; full replenish ~24h (longer if eccentric damage)
  • O₂ deficit = anaerobic energy at onset while aerobic system lags; EPOC = O₂ above rest to restore homeostasis (the two are related but NOT equal)
  • EPOC is intensity-, duration-, and mode-dependent; intensity has the greatest effect — heavy resistance > circuit training
  • Cycle ergometry: anaerobic dominates efforts up to ~60s; beyond ~60s aerobic progressively becomes primary (roughly 50/50 at ~60s)
Visual glossary
Energy continuum
The always-on blend of phosphagen, glycolytic, and oxidative ATP supply, with the dominant contributor shifting by intensity (primary) and duration (secondary).
Rate vs capacity
Inverse trade-off: the faster a system makes ATP, the less total ATP it can make. Phosphagen is highest-rate/lowest-capacity; fat oxidation is the reverse.
Substrate depletion
Selective using-up of fuels during exercise. Phosphagen and glycogen depletion cause fatigue; FFAs, lactate, and amino acids rarely limit performance.
Oxygen deficit
The anaerobically-supplied energy at the start of exercise, before aerobic ATP production ramps up to meet demand.
EPOC
Excess post-exercise oxygen consumption — O₂ uptake above resting used to restore homeostasis (PCr/ATP resynthesis, O₂ stores, temperature, circulation). Scales mainly with intensity.
PCr repletion
Aerobic rebuild of phosphocreatine after intense work: ~50% in 30s, full in ~8min — the reason short maximal efforts need generous rest.
Work-to-rest ratio
Interval prescription that biases a target system: ~1:12-1:20 for phosphagen (5-10s work), ~1:3-1:5 for fast glycolysis (15-30s work), and low ratios (near ~1:1) for the oxidative system (>3min work).
Maximal anaerobic capacity
The total energy the anaerobic mechanisms can supply during an all-out effort — the anaerobic contribution to short maximal exercise.

Connection to your training

Your Hyrox stations tax all three systems at once — sled push/pull sits on phosphagen+fast glycolysis, the 8×1 km runs on oxidative-glycolytic, wall balls and burpees on a mixed floor. Because full PCr rebuild is aerobic and takes up to ~8 min, program true max-power work (heavy sled, sprints) with long work:rest and never let glycogen bottom out — feed 0.7-3.0 g CHO/kg post-session for 24h refill. On race day the effort is continuous, so your ceiling is aerobic capacity and lactate clearance, not raw phosphagen power.
