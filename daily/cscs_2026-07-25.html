Polarized training & intensity distribution (Seiler)

Elite endurance athletes cluster ~75-85% of SESSIONS in zone 1 (below LT1/VT1, conversational) and ~15-20% in zone 3 (above LT2/VT2), with little in the 'grey zone' between — a polarized distribution. The model is defined by session count, not minutes. The competing models are pyramidal (most easy, some threshold, little hard) and threshold (heavy zone-2/sweet-spot emphasis). Polarized maximizes aerobic stimulus while limiting autonomic/cardiac cost, so high-intensity days stay genuinely hard. Most over-trained age-groupers fail by drifting easy days into the grey zone.

Key facts to memorize
  • Polarization is a SESSION-COUNT ratio (~80/20), not a minute-share
  • 3 zones: Z1 < LT1 (easy), Z2 between thresholds (grey), Z3 > LT2 (hard)
  • Grey-zone drift = too hard to recover, too easy to drive top-end
  • Polarization Index = log10(Z1%/Z3% × ... ) — track distribution over a block
  • Easy days must be truly easy so hard days can be truly hard
Visual glossary
Polarized distribution
~80% of sessions easy (below LT1), ~20% hard (above LT2), minimal middle. Defined by session count.
Grey zone
Between LT1 and LT2 — too hard to be recovery, too easy to maximize top-end. Where junk miles live.
Polarization Index
A single number summarizing how polarized a training block's intensity distribution is.
Connection to your training

Your Seiler 80/20 is computed on session count (not minutes) and you track a Polarization Index. Your Z2 HR cap of 132 indoor keeps easy days below LT1=147 — exactly the discipline that protects your hard 4×8 days.

HIIT & interval prescription (long intervals, supra-MLSS)

High-intensity intervals manipulate work duration, intensity, recovery duration and recovery intensity to target a system. For raising threshold/VO₂max, longer reps (4-8 min) at supra-MLSS with short recoveries accumulate the most time near VO₂max while staying repeatable. The first rep should feel almost too easy — pacing is the skill. Recovery is set to keep the SERIES on target, not to fully recover. The aim of threshold/VO₂max intervals is supra-MLSS metabolic stress, not all-out efforts that fail early.

Key facts to memorize
  • Variables: work duration, work intensity, recovery duration, recovery intensity
  • Long reps (4-8 min) supra-MLSS maximize time near VO₂max
  • Rep 1 should feel too easy — even pacing beats heroic fades
  • Recovery keeps the SERIES on target, not full recovery
  • Threshold/VO₂max work is controlled supra-MLSS, not all-out sprints
Visual glossary
Supra-MLSS
Intensity above maximal lactate steady state — lactate accumulates, forcing adaptation. The target of long intervals.
Work:rest ratio
Ratio of effort to recovery duration; for long aerobic intervals recovery only partially restores between reps.
Time near VO₂max
Total minutes spent close to maximal oxygen uptake — the key dose for VO₂max intervals.
